My Favorite Mangas

If you’re a fan of immersive stories and stunning artwork, here are some manhwa recommendations you’ll enjoy:


1.

Operation: True love

By: kkokkalee, Dledumb


Su-ae Shim has been dating her indifferent boyfriend, Minu Kang, for years and knows how hard it is to be with someone who won’t give her the time of day. She often wishes she were more like her confident stepsister, Ra-im. Things take a strange turn when Su-ae finds a sentient flip phone named Jellypop in her locker. Jellypop has plenty to say about her love life, especially as Su-ae grows distant from Minu and starts encountering his friend, Eunhyeok, more often. With rising doubts about Minu and Ra-im’s relationship, Su-ae may need a miracle to handle the rollercoaster of high school romance.

“The fate of the person loved by everyone is no different than the fate of the person loved by no one…” — Haru


2.

Romance 101

By: Namsoo


Bareum’s planner may be packed, but her diary is missing one thing—a date. When her friend’s attractive coworker convinces her to join a programming club, it seems like love might finally make it onto her schedule. But with her inexperience holding her back and constant run-ins with his moody friend, her plans might not go as smoothly as she hoped.

“What reason does anybody need to like someone? They just slowly soak into your heart like rain.” — Yuyeon Na


3.

Maybe Meant to Be

By: honeyskein, damcho


Jia Han, a 32-year-old jobless freelancer, is fed up with her parents' constant pressure to marry and move out. In a chance meeting with her childhood friend Mincheol, who faces the same issue, she jokingly suggests they get married—only for him to agree. Suddenly, Jia is thrown into an unexpected marriage, leading to a surprising and heartwarming romance.

“If you find their eating habits appealing, it’s a sign of affection.” — Jia Han
